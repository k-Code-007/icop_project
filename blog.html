Story #1 Artificial Intelligence and a Bike

What can a single shot of a bicycle say from a photogrammetric point of view? Nothing! Traditional photogrammetry wants to have at least a couple of overlapping images, a calibrated camera, a GPS, an inertial system ... Then it might say something...

What can a neural network say from a single snapshot of a bicycle? Much. The neural network (AI, Deep Learning) allows us to assert that the picture shows a bike. It detects the presence of a front and rear wheel, as well as performs image segmentation, i.e., selects fragments of the image that geometrically correspond to a particular object. Fig. 2 shows the results of recognition and segmentation using the popular YOLO900 platform.

What can a complex of neural network and an advanced photogrammetric procedure (“smart photogrammetry”!) do with a single image of a bicycle? So many! Smart photogrammetry can calculate distances, areas and volumes, speeds, and accelerations. Provide quantitative estimates of the probabilities of collision of moving objects, establish the similarities and differences of objects, calibrate video cameras, and much more. And smart photogrammetry can do all this with very modest processing and photographic resources. A regular smartphone is fine. Of course, if it is possible to have more than one snapshot, i.e. this is only a plus. The more shots, the deeper the results and the higher the accuracy. However, today we are just getting started. And we begin with a bicycle and with a single shot.
